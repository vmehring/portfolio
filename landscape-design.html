Landscape Design Portfolio

I feel fortunate to live in a neighborhood where I can watch wildlife. In our cultural shorthand, the animals who visit my yard might not seem "wild" compared to the critters that visited my childhood home in rural Pennsylvania.* I certainly don't see deer, foxes, or turkeys in Seattle. Nature, however, is everywhere, including urban environemnts. It exists on a spectrum of different ecosystems: dry to wet, warm to cold, hilly to flat, urban to rural and beyond. There is nowhere that nature doesn't exist, just as there is nowhere on Earth that is completely unaltered by humans.

Nature can successfully exist in urban environments. It looks different from the way it does is areas with smaller human populations, and it needs special care in order to truly thrive. My goals as a landscape designer include finding ways to make this happen.

* On the other hand, I didn't see salmon in Pennsylvania, so there's that.
