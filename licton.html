Licton Springs Park

This was a capstone project for completing the UW Certificate in restoration ecology. It was a design-build project that involved consultation with Friends of Licton Springs Park, a local advocacy group, as well as assistance from the Seattle Department of Parks and Recreation.

Our design team researched the history, current usage, and ecology of Licton Springs Park. We selected an area around the creek and up to the west edge of the park for treatment. The first step was removal of invasive plants. Holly and blackberry were pulled, and reed canary grass along the stream was mowed by SDPR. A double layer of cardboard was laid down to act as a weed barrier, and topped with four to six inches of mulch. More large woody debris - logs and branches - were added to the site. After the cardboard had softened, native trees and shrubs were planted, and willow stakes were planted along the stream.
